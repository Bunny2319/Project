Masala Dosa

Masala dosa is an iconic and widely popular South Indian dish consisting of a savory, crispy rice and lentil crepe stuffed with a spiced potato and onion filling. It is a common breakfast item that is enjoyed throughout India and globally, typically served with sambar and various chutneys.

Rava Uttpam

Rava uttapam, also known as suji uttapam, is a popular, instant South Indian breakfast item similar to a thick, savory pancake made from a semolina (rava/suji) and yogurt (curd) batter. Unlike traditional uttapam which requires overnight fermentation of rice and lentil batter, this version is quick to prepare and can be ready in about 30 minutes, including resting time.

Idli

Idli sambar is a classic and healthy South Indian meal consisting of soft, fluffy steamed rice and lentil cakes (idlis) served with a hearty, flavorful, and protein-packed lentil and mixed vegetable stew (sambar). It is a popular breakfast dish, often served with coconut chutney.

Pav Bhaji

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian fast food and street food dish that originated in Mumbai, consisting of a spiced, thick mashed vegetable curry (bhaji) served with a soft, buttered bread roll (pav).

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular and delicious dish from North India. It is a combination of spicy, flavorful chickpea curry (chole) and soft, fluffy, deep-fried leavened bread (bhature). It's a staple in Punjabi cuisine and is often enjoyed as a hearty breakfast, lunch, or snack

Samosa Chaat

Samosa chaat is a popular Indian street food made of broken crispy samosas topped with chickpea curry, various chutneys, and fresh garnishes like yogurt, chopped onions, tomatoes, and sev. It offers a balance of spicy, tangy, and sweet flavors and diverse textures in every bite.

Dahi puri

Dahi puri is a popular Indian street food and a type of chaat that originated in Mumbai. It consists of crispy, puffed mini-puri shells filled with a savory mixture and topped generously with yogurt and various chutneys and spices.
